# Concepts - Core Knowledge Base

> Mental models, frameworks, and abstract ideas.

## Active Concepts

### 80/20 Rule (Pareto Principle)
- **Definition**: 20% effort → 80% results
- **Application**: Find the vital few actions that drive most outcomes
- **Source**: Vilfredo Pareto
- **Related**: [[bottleneck-analysis]], [[leverage]]

### Bottleneck Analysis
- **Definition**: Constraint identification in any system
- **Question**: "What's slowing everything down?"
- **Application**: Always fix the bottleneck first
- **Related**: [[theory-of-constraints]], [[80-20-rule]]

### Compounding
- **Definition**: Small gains accumulating exponentially
- **Key**: Consistency > intensity
- **Application**: Knowledge, relationships, wealth, health
- **Related**: [[habits]], [[systems-thinking]]

### Opportunity Cost
- **Definition**: Value of the next best alternative foregone
- **Question**: "What am I giving up by doing this?"
- **Related**: [[decision-making]], [[trade-offs]]

### Second-Order Thinking
- **Definition**: Thinking about consequences of consequences
- **Question**: "And then what happens?"
- **Application**: Avoiding unintended consequences
- **Related**: [[systems-thinking]], [[inversion]]

### Inversion
- **Definition**: Solving by considering the opposite
- **Question**: "How do I guarantee failure?" → Avoid those
- **Related**: [[problem-solving]], [[mental-models]]

### Systems Thinking
- **Definition**: Seeing interconnections, not isolated events
- **Key**: Feedback loops, delays, stocks and flows
- **Related**: [[second-order-thinking]], [[holism]]

### Recurring Revenue > One-Time
- **Definition**: Prefer income streams over lump sums
- **Why**: Predictability, scalability, compound growth
- **Application**: SaaS, subscriptions, retainers
- **Related**: [[business-models]], [[financial-freedom]]

### Automation > Manual
- **Definition**: Systems that run without daily intervention
- **Key**: Invest time once, save time forever
- **Related**: [[leverage]], [[scalability]]

### Reversibility
- **Definition**: Can this decision be undone?
- **Rule**: Reversible = fast decision, Irreversible = slow + careful
- **Related**: [[decision-making]], [[risk-assessment]]
